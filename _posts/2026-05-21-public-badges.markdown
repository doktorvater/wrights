The first range of badges correspond to the realms supported by the local hand. In our case, these are rope, impact, toys, obedience, and bondage. The awarding judges pick the badge to represent ascendancy to journeyperson status in the realm. The badge and the overall realm display must be safe for work without embarassment or scandal, as well as obvious to hand members in terms of which realms the display represents. Badges can be individually different among the members in the hand, as long as the meaning is clear. So, ropes can be represented by a knotted rope badge, impact by a hand, hammer, or leather item, etc. The realm badges on public display must be difficult to interpret as representing kink, including the overall effect. So, for example, handcuffs or other criminal justice imagery may be approved on their own but in combination with other badges might make it too likely for the public to guess an interest in kink.

The realm badges of the first range are to be displayed publicly at hand events on a jacket, vest, or bag. Members may choose to wear their badges at other times in public if they wish. An apprentice can receive a badge for all levels and skills as a giver and a receiver. This is to be represented by displaying the badge on the left side of the front of the body for experience gained in doing things, while the right side of the front of the body is for badges that show approved advancements whilst being on the receiving end of approved kink achievements. This layout will make it difficult for the badges to work on a sash or fanny pack. The bag or clothing used for the public badges must work to cover the kink skill badges displayed on an article of clothing worn underneath and displayed at private hand events.

Normal practice is to display the public badges in arriving at a hand event and removing the bag or clothing when the event starts to reveal the kink achievements underneath. These skill badges may only be displayed at official kinkwright events.
