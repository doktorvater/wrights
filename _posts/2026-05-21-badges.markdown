If accepted as an apprentice, you must immediately commit at that meeting to earn the first rope badge and thereby advance to compagnon status in the hand. The rope badge must look the same as a badge worn by any hand member or else be approved by the members at the meeting where you were accepted. The first badge should only make a visual reference to rope or knots, without suggesting sex or kink. 

Badges are always to be displayed publicly at hand events on a jacket, shirt, or vest. Members may choose to wear their badges at other times in public if they wish. A member can receive a badge for all levels and skills as either a giver or a receiver. This is to be represented by displaying a badge on the left side of the front of the body for experience gained in doing things, while the right side of the front of the body is for badges that show advancement in having kink done to you. The two aspects of the same skill will be represented by an identical badge on both sides of the chest. Small differences in the type of activity may warrant different badges. Identical badges may be attached to a bag used to bring equipment to hand events, in the same layout as that used on your clothing. 

As with the first badge, your choice of a badge different from any of the ones worn in your hand means that you must bring the badge to your commitment meeting and have it approved by a majority of those compagnons attending the meeting.

If you are at a public event with another hand member to have that person witness a skill achievement, both of you must be wearing your hand badges during the event. 

Wearing any badge in public that suggests rope or kink without earning that badge from the hand, and having it approved by a majority vote of the compagnons present at the meeting, will result in that member being kicked out of the group.
