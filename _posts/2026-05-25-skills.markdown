Skill badges may be patches or pins. They must be selected in imitation of compagnons of the hand or approved at your commitment meeting. Each badge acknowledges that you have earned recognition for learning kink skills. The badges generally start with a broad area and then get more specific, but you can earn multiple badges, such as getting a suspension badge and following that up with chest harnesses and then hip harnesses. Many compagnons follow their rope badge with a badge for floor work, one for suspension, and then perhaps one for rope restraint, before specializing in any particular knots or patterns. 



Just as with the first badge, you must train for at least thirteen weeks and master the six steps before the badge can be approved. If a year has past since the commitment meeting, the members must vote to allow you to continue to train. This renewal is only allowed once.
